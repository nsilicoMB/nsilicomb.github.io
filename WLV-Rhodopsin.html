The Schematic display (Richardson diagram) details the location of helix and beta sheet regions. The color scheme indicates the hydrophobic (blue) and hydrophilic (red) areas of the molecule. By turning the Atom display style off, and rotating the model we can see that the beta sheet ribbon sections each have an arrow head at one end. This arrow points in the direction of the C-terminus.

Investigating H-bonding
Hydrogen bonds (H-bonds) play an important part in the formation of the secondary structure of proteins. In Fibrolase, H-bonds are involved in creating the coils and beta sheets in the protein. Select the Backbone type: "C-Alpha Stick" and then by turning on the H-bond monitor the hydrogen bonds appear as green dotted lines.

To better see where the H-bonds occur, and what their effect they have on the molecule's conformation, alter the display style to show only the secondary structure and the H-bonds. Now you can clearly see the hydrogen bonds among protein backbone's structure. If you want a better look, use the right mouse button to change the display properties and then zoom and rotate the model to study it further.
